Is the water safe? Blue-green algae across Victoria

Blue-green algae (cyanobacteria) blooms can turn lakes, reservoirs and rivers toxic — unsafe for swimming, and a real risk to pets and livestock that drink from them. Warnings are issued separately by many water authorities and councils, so this map gathers them into one place, and pairs the live picture with a decade of history so you can see which waters bloom, how often, and when.

Who to notify if you find a bloom
A lake, reservoir or channel

Contact the water corporation that manages it — Goulburn-Murray Water, GWMWater, Lower Murray Water, Southern Rural Water or Melbourne Water — or your local council for a town lake. They monitor and issue the public warnings.

Dead fish, pollution or an environmental emergency

Call EPA Victoria's 24-hour pollution hotline on 1300 372 842.

Worried about health, or your drinking water

The Department of Health's Water Unit — 1300 761 874 — advises on health risks and administers the Safe Drinking Water Act 2003.

A farm dam or private water on your land

Managing it is the landholder's responsibility. Keep stock and dogs away, and seek advice from Agriculture Victoria or your vet if animals are affected.

Not sure who manages the water?

DEECA coordinates Victoria's response through regional algal groups under the Blue-Green Algae Circular. Start with the water corporation for your area, or your council.
